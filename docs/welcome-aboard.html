principles 📡

Inspired by 37signals, this is a living list of the ideas that guide me:

  1. Always persist

    Maybe not always down the same path, but always in the same direction: forward.

  2. Maintain an internal locus of control

    You have absolute control over how you respond to the world around you.

  3. Less is more

    In speech and in design.

  4. Support the tribe

    Always work to better yourself and those around you, never tear them down or undermine them. Give wins to the group, accept individual responsibility when things go wrong.

  5. Respect the Lindy Effect

    Wikipedia for reference. This works both positively and negatively. The negative form: the longer a task remains undone, the longer it can be expected to remain undone.

  6. Have compassion

    There's not enough of it in day to day interactions. I like this quote by Henry James:

    “Three things in human life are important:
    the first is to be kind;
    the second is to be kind;
    and the third is to be kind.”

  7. Operate transparently

    My default is to be open and trusting of others. If we're negotiating compensation or working together, I don't want to hold any information back - I'd prefer to collaborate and have each party come away feeling better for the interaction. Plus, it's just easier not to play games or be sneaky.
